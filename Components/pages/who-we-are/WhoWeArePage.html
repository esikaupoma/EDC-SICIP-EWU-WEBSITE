Message of the Director

The Skills for Industry Competitiveness and Innovation Program (SICIP), managed by the Government of Bangladesh, has partnered with East West University through a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) to establish the Executive Development Center (EDC). This center is entrusted with implementing advanced training programs aimed at developing skilled executives, entrepreneurs, and business leaders across priority industrial sectors.

Over the course of the program, EDC is expected to develop a highly skilled workforce, ready to be engaged as mid- to top-level executives or entrepreneurs, thus supporting the growth and competitiveness of Bangladesh’s industrial sectors.

The Executive Development Center (EDC) at East West University, with the exclusive financial support of the Government of Bangladesh, plays a pivotal role in fostering industry-academia collaboration and delivering practical, experience-based training to enhance skills and productivity.

The EDC’s training programs are designed based on rigorous industry needs assessment and continuous collaboration with sector stakeholders. The curricula include a combination of generic and industry-specific courses, along with internships and hands-on learning opportunities. Course content is regularly updated through feedback from industry experts, market demands, and participant input.

In summary, the Graduate Diploma in Leather, Leather Goods, and Footwear Management (GDLFM) program aims to produce mid-level managers and entrepreneurs who will make significant contributions to the leather and footwear sector, thereby supporting the economic growth and innovation capacity of Bangladesh’s priority industrial sectors.
